Project Overview

This project involved participating in The Fresh Connection, a team-based supply chain simulation in which each round represented two weeks of operational decision-making across an orange juice production and distribution company. The simulation spanned eight rounds and was divided into two phases: Phase 1 (Rounds 1-4) focused on experimenting and learning how different decisions affected performance, while Phase 2 (Rounds 5-8) emphasized executing a refined strategy based on what we learned.

Our team‚Äôs primary objective was to maximize Return on Investment (ROI) by aligning operational, purchasing, sales, and supply chain decisions while minimizing costs, obsolescence, and operational inefficiencies. Performance outcomes were evaluated after each round using financial and operational KPIs.

Tools & Skills

  • Supply chain simulation strategy with KPI-driven decision-making across 8 rounds (2-week cycles).
  • Operations planning: balancing capacity, labor, throughput, and quality to support ROI goals.
  • Cross-functional collaboration across purchasing, operations, sales, and supply chain.
  • Continuous improvement through iterative testing, outcome analysis, and strategy refinement.
  • Executive decision communication through round debriefs and team alignment discussions.

Simulation Roles & Responsibilities

  • VP Purchasing: component sourcing, supplier negotiations, supply terms, and pricing.
  • VP Operations (my role): production and warehouse operations - capacity, staffing, and process improvements.
  • VP Sales: customer negotiations, delivery terms, and demand alignment.
  • VP Supply Chain Management: supply chain strategy, stock planning, and safety stock deployment.

My Role as VP of Operations

As Vice President of Operations, I was responsible for managing production capacity, warehouse operations, labor planning, and process reliability. My decisions directly affected production plan adherence, warehousing costs, labor utilization, and overall Return on Investment (ROI).

Key responsibilities and decision levers included:

  • Managing warehouse capacity (inbound and outbound pallets) and throughput constraints.
  • Setting permanent staffing levels for inbound and outbound operations.
  • Planning bottling line operations, including shift scheduling and throughput adjustments.

Operational improvement actions:

  • Implementing SMED bottling actions to reduce changeover time.
  • Enabling bottling speed increases to improve throughput.
  • Managing raw material inspection to improve input quality.
  • Reducing inbound intake time to flatten labor peaks.
  • Coordinating staff training for faster breakdown resolution.
  • Implementing preventive maintenance to improve bottling reliability.

Phase 2 operational upgrades (starting Round 5):

  • Outsourcing outbound warehouse operations.
  • Adopting inflatable PET bottles to reduce space and labor requirements.

Throughout the simulation, I collaborated closely with the VP of Sales and VP of Supply Chain to align capacity decisions with safety stock levels, shelf life targets, and demand forecasts.

Reflection

This simulation strengthened my understanding of how operational decisions directly affect both supply chain performance and financial outcomes. Serving as VP of Operations required balancing real constraints such as warehouse capacity, staffing levels, production throughput, quality controls, and process reliability, while working closely with teammates in purchasing, sales, and supply chain management.

Through Phase 1 experimentation and Phase 2 execution, I learned how to evaluate results from each round, identify operational bottlenecks, and implement targeted improvements based on data rather than assumptions. Overall, this project reinforced the importance of cross-functional alignment and KPI-driven decision making when optimizing supply chain performance and improving return on investment.
